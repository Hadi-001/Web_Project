Common Symptoms of Depression

Depression is a mental health condition that affects how a person feels, thinks, and handles daily activities. Here are some common symptoms:

  • Persistent Sadness: A feeling of sadness or emptiness that lasts for weeks.
  • Loss of Interest: No longer enjoying activities that were once pleasurable.
  • Fatigue: Feeling tired all the time, even with enough rest.
  • Changes in Sleep Patterns: Trouble falling asleep, staying asleep, or sleeping too much.
  • Appetite Changes: Eating too much or too little, leading to weight gain or loss.
  • Difficulty Concentrating: Struggling with focus, memory, and decision-making.
  • Feelings of Guilt or Worthlessness: Self-blame, excessive guilt, or low self-esteem.
  • Physical Symptoms: Unexplained aches, pains, or digestive issues.
  • Suicidal Thoughts: In severe cases, thoughts of self-harm or suicide.
