Common Symptoms of Anxiety

Anxiety disorders affect millions of people worldwide. While occasional anxiety is normal, chronic anxiety can interfere with daily life. Here are some common symptoms:

  • Excessive Worrying: Persistent worry that is difficult to control.
  • Restlessness: Feeling on edge, nervous, or unable to relax.
  • Fatigue: Constant tiredness, even after a good night's sleep.
  • Difficulty Concentrating: Trouble focusing due to excessive worry.
  • Increased Heart Rate: Rapid heartbeat or palpitations without physical exertion.
  • Shortness of Breath: Feeling like you can't catch your breath.
  • Sleep Problems: Trouble falling or staying asleep due to anxious thoughts.
  • Digestive Issues: Stomach pain, nausea, or irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).
  • Panic Attacks: Sudden episodes of intense fear or discomfort.
